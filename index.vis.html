Welcome to Local Wildlife Tracker

Wildlife Tracker is designed to empower communities to observe, protect and support local animal populations and their habitats. By providing tools to report wildlife sightings, track species health and changes in animal behavior or habitat conditions, our platform encourages conservation and engagement with the natural world.

Our goal is to increase awareness of local biodiversity, promote and educate the public about the vital role animals have in maintaining a balanced ecosystem. Whether you're a student, volunteer, or concerned neighbor, Wildlife Tracker offers the knowledge and support needed to make informed decisions that help protect and preserve local wildlife.

The Serengeti-Mara Ecosystem: A Case Study

This visualization is based on real data from the Serengeti-Mara ecosystem, one of the most iconic wildlife regions on Earth. While conservation efforts have helped protect parts of this area, surrounding regions have faced severe degradation.

Climate change, agricultural expansion, and poaching have reduced wildlife populations by over 70% in some areas since 1990. Without immediate action, experts warn that entire ecosystems could collapse within decades, taking thousands of species with them.

Why Animal Wildlife Matters

Animals are essential to healthy ecosystems. They help control pests, pollinate plants, disperse seeds and maintain the balance of nature.

Protecting animal populations ensures biodiversity, strengthens resilience against environmental changes, and keeps urban and rural ecosystems functioning properly. Every species, no matter how small, plays a critical role - this makes it crucial for communities to raise awareness as animals play a vital role to sustain a healthy ecosystem.

Wildlife in Our Communities

Even in urban areas like London, diverse animal species thrive in parks, gardens and green spaces. Foxes, hedgehogs, birds, and insects are indicators of healthy ecosystems. By observing wildlife, communities can identify areas that need protection and help promote coexistence between animals and humans, developing a healthy ecosystem for the future.
